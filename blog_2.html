LIFE-ILLUMINATED AMBASSADOR

The Rural Vulnerable Reconstruction Program in Nepal

Syangja, Nepal
2016.7 - 2016.8

In the summer of 2016, I participated in the Rural Vulnerable Reconstruction Program in Nepal with teacher's recommendation. It was that summer that prompted me to establish my vision of connecting people around the world.




Chihmou Hsieh, a professor at National Taiwan Normal University, has been involved in transformative learning and flipped education. In 2013, after witnessing the challenges faced by the local community in Nepal, he initiated the Rock Project. The project aims to empower residents to achieve self-sufficiency within seven to nine years while improving the quality of education and public health.


The Nepal Illuminating Life English Primary School, situated in the mountainous region near Pokhara, was established by a 103-year-old Christian grandfather who donated land and was raised by the Taiwan Christian Church. At present, the school has over 120 students. During my involvement in the project, the school building was still under construction, with only the church and some classrooms completed We were assigned to teach the children basic subjects in English. And teach them to express themselves through art.




The local living conditions are not ideal. We lived with an unstable electricity supply and limited water sources, I had to bathe in rainwater or streams and sleep by candlelight.


The most touching moment for me was during a home visit when the student's mother gifted us with steamed corn. However, I later realized that one corn might be their food for the day. Although some locals cannot speak English, I communicated through facial-bodily expressions. This inspired me that language is not the sole interactive interface; there are other media to express meanings and emotions.

It takes us three days to travel from Kathmandu to Syangja, changing three modes of transportation. Each morning, I woke up under the Himalayan mountains, feeling an overwhelming sense of inner happiness. In this small place, I discovered simplicity and kindness. I also reflected on how satisfied I am. These experiences inspired my revolution of the social interface - an interconnected virtual world where every lonely soul and minority group can share their stories, bridging gaps between space and time, and fostering a global community of understanding.
