It is my Capstone Project at NCKU Architecture. Participating in the RATs｜Robotic Arm Tectonics studio (directed by Prof. Yang-Ting Shen) and RAC-Coon｜Robot Aided Creation and Construction (directed by Mr. Chia-Ching Yen). We built a full-scale wooden pavilion using industrial robot arms to fabricate standard lumbers. The robot is equipped with spindles as a 6-axis CNC milling machine for realizing multi-dimensional drilling technology. This project intergrated Building Information Modeling and Parametric Design and with Grasshopper PRC in Visualized Coding for Robot Control. We believe that innovatitive and feasible smart construction methods will emerge from this prototype.

We utilized Rhinocero 3D as a simulation integration platform, in which we developed the design process, the accurate corresponding 3D model, and the movement paths of robotic arm, allowing us to simulate the operation of construction and the precise figuration of every component. Moreover, we utilized Grasshopper to generate a Voronoi-pattern roof and scripted a tree-bionic algorithm in Python to manipulate parameters, including the number of branches, branch angles, and interval heights.
